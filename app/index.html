Where is this all happening?

Everything is happening in beautiful Sonoma county california in a small town named Guerneville. The wedding itself will take place in Armstrong National Park among the awe inspiring red wood trees. Once the ceremony is completed we will be moving to the Rio Nido Roadhouse, about a ten minute drive from the forest.

Where should I stay?

There are a lot of options in the area, as this is a popular tourist spot for San Franciscans. Many of the available options are houses for rent and vacation properties. We recommend getting a group together and staying in a comfortable house with all the emmenities. We are looking into group rates for housing but here are some options for now: airbnb, The Rio Nido Lodge, Boon Hotel+Spa, Creekside Inn, The R3 Hotel

Flying

The wedding is about an hour north of San Francisco. This area has 2 reasonable airport options in San Francisco (SFO) and Oakland (OAK). It is advised to rent a car to get to Guerneville.

What To Pack

Northern California in March can be a mixed bag. The weather could be between 50°f and 80°f so pack accordingly. Hopefully the draught breaks and we will get some rain, so a rain coat and umbrella is advised.

The wedding itself will be outdoors followed by a party at a bar. We recommend wearing comfortable shoes that you can do a little off roading in (that means you Mom). It is pretty well shaded so hats are not needed but who doesn't love a stylish hat. We are pretty casual so we don't expect you to dress up.

Recreation

There is certainely no lack of things to do in the area. Sonoma county is close to the ocean, on iconic highway 1, and full of wineries. Here is a short list we came up with: Go to the coast, drive or bike Highway 1, hike Armstrong Woods, visit Point Reyes National Sea Shore, do the Korbel tour, go mountain biking in Fairfax; the home of Mountain biking, spend some time in San Francisco, tour a winerie in Napa Valley, or Sonoma
